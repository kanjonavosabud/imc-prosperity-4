Fee formula: fee = (volume_pct/100)^2 * budget

  -> quadratic in volume

Constraints:

  where v_i = allocation fraction, r_i = expected return on traded volume.

PART 2: OPTIMAL PORTFOLIO FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

Per-asset optimization (ignoring budget constraint):
  PnL_i = v_i * B * r_i - v_i^2 * B
  d/dv_i = B * r_i - 2 * v_i * B = 0
  => v_i* = r_i / 2  (UNCONSTRAINED OPTIMUM)

  At v_i*: marginal next dollar earns 0
  Past v_i*: every additional dollar LOSES money in expectation

If sum(|v_i*|) > 1, apply Lagrangian:
  v_i = max(0, (|r_i| - lambda) / 2)
  Choose lambda so sum(v_i) = 1

For our format (B=$1M), optimal pct = 50r (where r is decimal return).


================================================================================
PART 3: WHY 73% BEATS 100% — THE QUADRATIC FEE TRAP
================================================================================

The fee is convex (parabolic), the gain is linear. There is a crossover where
they cancel — at v = r/2. Past that, the next dollar of trade ACTIVELY LOSES
money in expectation.

CONCRETE EXAMPLE — Thermalite at r=30%:
  Allocation v |  Gross gain  |    Fee    |   Net PnL
       10%     |   $30,000    |  $10,000  |   +$20,000
       15% (*) |   $45,000    |  $22,500  |   +$22,500   <- max
       20%     |   $60,000    |  $40,000  |   +$20,000
       25%     |   $75,000    |  $62,500  |   +$12,500
       30% (=r)|   $90,000    |  $90,000  |       $0     (break-even)
       40%     |  $120,000    | $160,000  |   -$40,000
       50%     |  $150,000    | $250,000  |  -$100,000

KEY INSIGHTS:
- Forcing Thermalite from optimal 15% up to 50% turns +$22.5k into -$100k
- Unused budget = 0 (zero opportunity cost)
- Over-allocated budget < 0 (negative marginal PnL past r/2)
- So unused budget BEATS over-allocated budget

When 100% is right:
- Only when sum(r_i/2) > 1 across all assets
- For N-asset equal portfolio: avg r >= 2/N
- N=1: need r=200%
- N=5: need avg r=40%
- N=9: need avg r=22%
- N=20: need avg r=10%

PART 5: r=100% MEANING (cleared up confusion)
================================================================================

r = price return on volume traded (asset's actual price move)

At r=100% (asset doubled):
  - v=1.0 forced: gross +$1M, fee $1M, NET = $0 (break-even)
  - v=0.5 optimal: gross +$500k, fee $250k, NET = +$250k

So r=100% does NOT mean we double our money on a forced 100% allocation —
it means we BREAK EVEN. The fee structure eats the entire gain.

The asset literally doubled; you just left it all on the table by oversizing.
